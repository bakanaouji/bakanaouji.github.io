I study methods for aligning large language models with human preferences. Through Best-of-N sampling, minimum Bayes risk (MBR) decoding, and filtering of preference data (DPO), I aim to improve output alignment while suppressing reward hacking.

## Key questions
- How can we improve alignment while suppressing reward hacking?
- How should the quality of preference data be evaluated and filtered?
- How do decoding and sampling strategies affect alignment?
